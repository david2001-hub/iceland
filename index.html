HISTORY OF THE FLAG

According to legend, the Danish king Valdemar II (1170-1241), on the eve of the battle of Lyndanisse, saw a white cross appear in the dark sky; the monarch interpreted this vision as a sign that Christ ordered him to slaughter the unhappy Estonians, which he did correctly the next day. From this legend was born the Scandinavian Cross, which is also carried by the flags of Denmark, Finland, Norway and Sweden. .
